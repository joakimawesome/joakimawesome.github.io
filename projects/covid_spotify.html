COVID-19 Lockdowns vs. Music Trends

As a music lover and data enthusiast, I'm eager to explore the relationship between COVID-19 lockdowns and people's musical preferences. With so many of us spending more time at home and seeking ways to cope with the pandemic, I thought it would be interesting to see if there have been any significant shifts in what people are listening to on Spotify. By examining the streaming data, we can identify trends and patterns that may reveal how lockdowns have impacted our musical tastes. With this information, We can gain a deeper understanding of how people are responding to the pandemic and find ways to enhance our overall well-being through music.

    • Potential Hypotheses

    Based on the global situation and the profound impact of the pandemic on people's lives, we expect to see the following trends in music preferences:

    1. Increase in Calm Genres: A spike in calming and instrumental music streams during strict lockdowns as a means to relax and destress.
    2. Dance and Energetic Music: A surge in energetic music streams as lockdowns ease, indicating optimism or increased social activities.
    3. Nostalgic Tracks: A potential resurgence in older or nostalgic songs as listeners reminisce about pre-pandemic times.
    4. Lyrics and Speechiness: Preference towards music with profound lyrics during significant pandemic milestones, reflecting societal sentiments.
    5. Global Event Influences: Shifts in music streams following major global or national pandemic-related announcements.

    Data

    The ideal dataset for music will inform us about music tastes each day, and place it side-by-side next to COVID-19 daily cases/deaths.
    I will define daily music tastes as the average of audio features of the top 200 songs trending Spotify Charts on a certain day.

    We obtain the daily top 100 as a downloadable csv file from Spotify Charts.

    Using each song's URI in the dataset, we obtain the following audio features provided by Spotify: acousticness, danceability, energy, instrumentalness, liveness, mode, speechiness, tempo, and valence. There are a few other features from Spotify that are not so relevant.

    The dataset contains details about each day from 01/01/2017 to 12/31/2022. Each day comprises 200 songs and the number of listens (or streams) each day has. The data transformation takes those 200 songs' Spotify URIs to retrieve their audio features, \( \text{a} \). Then the audio features are given a weighted average using the percent streamed in a day. Weighing the songs based on how much they were streamed offers a reliable measure of music tastes on a particular day. The formula for the weight of each song is:

    \[ \text{w}_i = \frac{\text{s}_ i}{\text{s}_{total}} \]

    Where \( \text{w}_i \) is the weight and \( \text{s}_i \) is the number of streams or listens for song \( i \).
    The final values for the audio features, \( \text{a} \), of the day is then the sum of the weighted features of all 200 songs:

    \[ \text{daily audio feature} = \text{x}_n = \sum_{i=1}^{200} \text{a}_i \text{w}_i \]

    These new values are meant to summarize the audio features of the day \( \text{n} \).

    Although Christmas holidays coming around the corner may be intuitive, we should also interpret the data with more thought about where it comes from.

    Spotify is the most popular music app in the U.S., and with such a massive user base, we expect that its accumulated data should reflect population preferences accurately.
